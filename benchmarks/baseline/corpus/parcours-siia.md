# Programme handbook — Parcours SIIA

## Admission requirements

Candidates hold a bachelor's degree in computer science, applied mathematics,
or an equivalent discipline. The selection committee reviews the academic
record, a short motivation letter, and one reference. Applications close on
15 March each year, and results are published in early May. Candidates from
outside the European Union must additionally supply a certified translation of
their transcripts.

## Course structure

The programme runs over four semesters. The first two cover foundations:
probability, optimisation, and statistical learning. The third semester is
devoted to specialisation, where students pick three electives from a list of
eleven. The fourth semester is entirely occupied by the final internship.

## Electives

Electives include natural language processing, computer vision, reinforcement
learning, distributed systems, and formal verification. Each elective carries
four credits. A student may substitute one elective for a supervised research
project, subject to approval by the programme director before the end of
September.

## The final internship

The internship lasts a minimum of sixteen weeks and must be carried out in a
host organisation approved by the department. Students submit a written report
of between forty and sixty pages, followed by an oral defence before a jury of
three. The internship is graded on the report, the defence, and an assessment
from the host supervisor, weighted 40/40/20.

## Graduation

A student graduates having accumulated one hundred and twenty credits with a
weighted average of at least ten out of twenty, no individual module below
seven, and a passing grade on the internship defence. Students who fail a
single module may resit it once, in September.
