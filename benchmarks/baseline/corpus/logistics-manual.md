# Warehouse operations manual

## Receiving

Inbound pallets are scanned at the dock and matched against the advance
shipping notice. A discrepancy of more than two percent by unit count triggers
a hold, and the pallet is moved to the quarantine bay pending a manual recount.
Recounts are performed by two operators independently.

## Storage

The Lyon facility operates narrow-aisle racking with a nominal capacity of
eleven thousand pallet positions. Fast-moving stock is held in the forward
pick face; slow movers are consolidated into the upper levels during the night
shift. Ambient temperature is maintained between fifteen and twenty-two
degrees.

## Picking and dispatch

Orders received before 14:00 ship the same day. Picking is wave-based, with
waves released every ninety minutes. Each wave is capped at four hundred order
lines to keep the packing benches from starving or flooding.

## Returns

Returned goods are inspected within two working days of arrival. Items in
resaleable condition rejoin available stock; damaged items are written off
against the monthly shrinkage allowance, which is capped at 0.4 percent of
throughput.

## Carrier network

The site ships across the European Union through three contracted carriers.
Consignments above thirty kilograms move on the pallet network; everything
else goes parcel. Customs documentation for non-EU destinations is raised
automatically from the order record.
