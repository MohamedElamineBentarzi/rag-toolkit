# Quarterly report — third quarter

## Revenue

Revenue for the third quarter reached 4.2 million euros, up from 3.75 million
in the comparable period a year earlier. Growth of twelve percent was driven
chiefly by the subscription tier, which now accounts for just over half of all
recurring income. Perpetual licences continued their slow decline.

## Costs

Operating costs rose to 3.1 million euros. The largest single increase was in
cloud infrastructure, up 340 thousand euros year on year, attributable to the
migration of the analytics workload onto managed instances. Headcount costs
were flat.

## Regional performance

The German market grew fastest at nineteen percent. France was flat. The
Nordic region contracted by four percent, which management attributes to the
loss of a single large account in Sweden rather than to any broader trend.

## Outlook

Management expects full-year revenue between 16.5 and 17.2 million euros. The
guidance assumes no further currency movement and excludes the effect of the
pending acquisition, which has not yet cleared regulatory review.

## Risks

The concentration of recurring income in a small number of enterprise accounts
remains the principal risk. The five largest customers together represent
thirty-one percent of subscription revenue. A prolonged outage of the managed
analytics platform would also carry material contractual penalties.
